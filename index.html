Church History Study Guide

The First Vision
1820

A young boy named Joseph Smith was concerned for the welfare of his soul and wondered which church was correct. In response to his prayer, God the Father and Jesus Christ appeared to him.

The Book of Mormon
1830

Joseph Smith was led to the preserved record of ancient peoples from the Americas. By the gift and power of God, he translated the book which we have today as another testament of Jesus Christ.

Priesthood Restoration
1830 - Present

God gave power to ancient prophets and apostles, and in modern times sent many of these disciples as angels to Joseph Smith and other church leaders to bring His power back to the earth.

The Doctrine and Covenants
First Published 1835

Before the official organization of the church and throughout his lifetime, the prophet Joseph received and recorded revelations the Lord desired to give to the modern Church.

Additional Scripture
The Pearl of Great Price, Joseph Smith's Bible Translation

In addition to the Book of Mormon, the Lord brought forth additional scripture to further establish the doctrine of the Church and instruct the prophet Joseph.

The Law of Consecration
-

Part of the restoration of the gospel of Jesus Christ included the restoration of this law, in which Saints are commanded to consecrate their time, talents, and means to the kingdom of God.

Kirtland Revelations
Afterlife, Word of Wisdom

Joseph Smith received significant revelations during his time in Kirtland, including a vision about the afterlife, the Word of Wisdom, and initial guidance about the endowment ordinance.

Kirtland & Missouri Trouble
-

Difficulties in Kirtland led to many becoming disaffected from the Church. As Saints tried to gather in Missouri, they faced additional difficulties, including the infamous extermination order.

Nauvoo
1839 - 1846

Saints recovering from expulsion from Missouri found refuge in Illinois, purchased land, and built up Nauvoo. Saints built a temple in Nauvoo, and the Relief Society was organized.

Plural Marriage
-

Plural marriage (polygamy) remains a difficult part of Church history for members of the church. Learning all we can about its practice can shed light on this commandment from the Lord.
