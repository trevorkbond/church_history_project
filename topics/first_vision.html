Church History Study Guide

The First Vision

Overview

In 1820, a young boy named Joseph Smith was becoming increasingly concerned about the salvation of his soul. Questions about religion were not uncommon for someone in his time and place to have. Many events had led to Joseph's questions, including the Second Great Awakening. This was a time of religious revival in the United States. Preachers of various religious sects travelled around the country seeking converts. Joseph lived in western New York, an area that became known as the burned over district because there were so many preachers who had held meetings there. Later, Joseph described how he became increasingly concerned with the topic of religion, and sought to join a church and have spiritual experiences like he had heard others describe. This culminated in Joseph Smith seeking answers - he turned to the Bible and eventually to God in prayer. After travelling to what is now called the Sacred Grove, Joseph prayed to God and was visited by God the Father and Jesus Christ.

What may not be known by many who have already heard of Joseph Smith's First Vision is that there are four primary accounts of it. They are as follows:

  • 1832 account - originally recorded in Joseph's journal/unpublished autobiography.
  • 1835 account - a transcript from Joseph telling a traveller to Kirtland about the vision.
  • 1838 account - most well-known account by modern Latter-day Saints, written as part of the History of the Church.
  • 1842 account - also known as the Wentworth letter, written to John Wentworth, an editor of a newspaper in Illinois.
