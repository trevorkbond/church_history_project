Church History Study Guide

The Doctrine and Covenants

Overview

The Lord had more to give the Saints of the restored Church than the Book of Mormon - He also guided the prophet Joseph through many revelations that were, over time, compiled and are today known as the Doctrine and Covenants. Revelations that Joseph received that are included in today's Doctrine and Covenants come from a wide variety of places and times in his lifetime; the earliest received revelation included in this book of scripture comes from 1823 when Joseph was first visited by Moroni.

As Joseph received revelations and had them recorded, they were not immediately put into a book. Some of these revelations were originally compiled and were known as the Book of Commandments and Revelations and the Kirtland Revelation Book (today they are known as the Manuscript Revelations Books 1 and 2). The first attempted publishing of Joseph's revelations was called The Book of Commandments. However, only 30 copies of this first edition remain today, as the printing press working on printing was attacked and leveled by vigilantes.

Later, these revelations were successfully printed as the Doctrine and Covenants of the Church of the Latter-day Saints. The "doctrine" part was meant to indicate teachings the Church gave members of the School of the Prophets. These are today known as the Lectures on Faith and can be seen as an early Church version of Preach My Gospel. The "covenants" part was meant to indicate Joseph's revelations. Today's Doctrine and Covenants no longer includes the Lectures on Faith, but it has retained the name regardless.
