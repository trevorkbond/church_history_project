Church History Study Guide

Nauvoo

Overview

Driven from Missouri and seeking refuge, the Saints fled to Missouri. They were met with warmth and help from locals in a town called Quincy. Later, they were offered land in a town originally named Commerce. The Saints purchased this land and renamed it Nauvoo, meaning the "beautiful city". Nauvoo saw eventual prosperity and many significant developments in Church history occurred here. One significant doctrine that was revealed to the prophet Joseph was the doctrine of proxy ordinance work. At the time, all that was revealed was the doctrine of baptisms for the dead (other proxy ordinances like endowments and sealings were not performed until after the Saints traveled to Utah).

Another significant happening in the Church in Nauvoo was the organization of the Relief Society, then known as the Nauvoo Female Relief Society. Joseph Smith was involved in its organization and "turned a key", meaning he exercised his priesthood keys and organized the Relief Society as an essential organization in the restored Church. Members of the Relief Society voted that Emma Smith be made their first President, and they held several meetings during their time in Nauvoo. Later, after the death of Joseph Smith, Brigham Young feared that Emma, who had struggled mightily with the revealed practice of plural marriage, might incite the members of the Relief Society against him and other Church leaders, so the Relief Society was discontinued for a time.

Before the prophet's martydrom, he taught and revealed critical doctrines and ordinances. One example is when he spoke at the funeral of King Follett about the true nature of God. Here, the prophet taught that God was once as man is, and that man can eventually become as God. The prophet also gave a relatively small group of Saints a presentation of the endowment that is more similar to what today's Latter-day Saints know it as than the endowment given in Kirtland. In this, the prophet taught the company the keys, signs, and tokens of the priesthood. Some in the company recognized that this presentation was similar to rites performed in Masonic lodges (more on this can be learned at the Church's article below).
