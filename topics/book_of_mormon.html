Church History Study Guide

The Book of Mormon

Overview

Joseph Smith was still a young boy when he was visited by Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ, however They alluded to the fact that They had a work for him to do. The first thing Joseph was to do in the beginnings of the restoration of the fulness of the gospel of Jesus Christ was find and translate a record of an ancient people who lived in the Americas. These people had the gospel of Jesus Christ revealed to them through prophets and heavenly manifestations. They were even visited by Jesus Christ after his crucifixion and resurrection. The Lord appeared to be appeasing one of Joseph Smith's concerns about the impossibility of proving the truthfulness of a church by an appeal to the Bible. With the Bible and the Book of Mormon, the Lord was able to bring back truths that had been lost through centuries of confoundment.

On the 21st of September, 1823, as he was retiring to bed, the young Joseph Smith was visited by an ancient prophet who had written part of the record that would become the Book of Mormon named Moroni. Moroni taught Joseph that God had a work for him to do, and instructed him about how he was to find and translate the record. However, Joseph still had much to learn before he was prepared to handle the record. Due to the poverty his family experienced, the lure of using an ancient record recorded on gold plates to help his family financially was powerful. It was four years after Moroni first visited Joseph that he was finally prepared to obtain the record.

Many members of the Church may be unaware of some of the specifics on how the Book of Mormon was translated. Most well known is that Joseph Smith used interpreters called the Urim and Thummim to look into to see translated English text. Not as well known is that, over time, it became convenient for Joseph to use a seer stone that he had found many years earlier. He would place this stone in a hat to block out outstide light, and then translated English text would appear to him, and he could dictate the translation to a scribe in the same room. Testimonies of how this translation process occurred abound from those who acted as scribe, and yet Joseph remained hesitant to reveal the specifics of translation, stating simply that it was done by the gift and power of God and that the Lord never intended for the world to know the specifics of the Book of Mormon's translation.
