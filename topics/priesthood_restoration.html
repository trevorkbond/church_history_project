Church History Study Guide

Priesthood Restoration

Overview

As Joseph Smith and Oliver Cowdery worked on the Book of Mormon translation, they came across a section that led them to wonder about God's priesthood authority. In 3 Nephi, they learned about how Christ conferred authority to baptize on His selected disciples. They wondered what implications this held for the modern day, and where the authority to baptize had gone. Seeking answers to these questions, they went to the nearby Susquehanna River in Pennsylvania to pray. As they prayed, they were visited by John the baptist, who conferred on them the Aaronic priesthood, which he taught held the keys of the ministering of angels, the gospel of repentance, and of baptism. After receiving this authority, Joseph and Oliver baptized each other.

The restoration of the Melchizedek priesthood and its keys, on the other hand, was not so much of a one time event, and it could be said that it is still ongoing as there are keys remaining to be restored to the earth. Though the exact time and place is not known, sometime several months after the restoration of the Aaronic priesthood, Joseph and Oliver were visited by Peter, James, and John, who conferred upon them the Melchizedek priesthood. Another significant Melchizedek priesthood restoration event was in the Kirtland temple, where Joseph and Oliver were visited by Moses, Elias, and Elijah, who gave restored the keys of the gathering of Israel, the dispensation of the gospel of Abraham, and the sealing power, respectively.
